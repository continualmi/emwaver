The Evolution of EMWaver Hardware

A chronological archive of the iterations of the EMWaver board. From breadboard prototypes to the polished v10, explore the design decisions, trade-offs, and lessons learned along the way.

Sep 2023

Emwaver v1.0: The Breadboard Proto

The very first proof-of-concept. This version proved that the ESP32-S3 could directly drive the CC1101 without a dedicated coprocessor. However, the antenna placement was suboptimal, causing significant interference with the USB data lines. The USB-Micro connector was also a common pain point.

Oct 2023

Emwaver v2.0: First SMD Attempt

This was our first attempt at a surface-mount design. We moved to a more compact form factor, but still struggled with RF noise, especially near the USB lines. The USB-Micro connector remained.

Jan 2024

Emwaver v5.0: The USB-C Leap

A major redesign saw the introduction of USB-C, solving the physical durability issues. We moved to a 4-layer PCB stackup to better isolate the RF signals, which significantly improved performance and reduced interference. This was also the first version to support the expansion header standard we use today.

4-Layer PCB USB-C Standard Expansion Header Improved RF Isolation

Oct 2024

Emwaver v10.0: Production Ready

The culmination of all previous iterations. This version features a highly optimized 4-layer PCB, refined antenna matching, and dedicated shielding to virtually eliminate RF interference. It includes all standard expansion headers and is fully production-ready, supporting all advanced Wavelet features.
