In this video we go through the full EMWaver lineup and the end-to-end manufacturing workflow: choosing the right board, ordering PCBs + assembly through JLCPCB, sourcing the parts you’ll need to buy separately, and (when available) printing 3D cases.

If you’re deciding what to build:
- iOS users: focus on the ESP32-S3 lineup (STM32 devices don’t support iPhone).
- Android users: both STM32 (USB) and ESP32 (BLE/Wi-Fi) are viable depending on what you want to do.
- Cost: low-volume PCBA has setup fees (minimum order is typically 2), so the flagship EMWaver is the most expensive build; Shield/DIY are great starting points on ESP32.

You’ll learn
- How to use the hardware catalog to open boards in EasyEDA and order PCBs + PCBA
- JLCPCB settings that matter (Qty 2, top-side assembly, confirm placement)
- Economic vs standard PCBA, and when you’re forced to switch
- How to review/fix BOM issues (missing parts, wrong values, IR receiver selection quirks)
- How to handle out-of-stock parts with global sourcing / “My Parts” inventory (antennas)
- What you must hand-solder / buy separately (headers, modules, antennas, dev boards)
- 3D printed casings: export/download STL → print → assembly screws
- Optional add-on modules (RC522, CC1101, NRF24) and quick prototyping with jumper wires
